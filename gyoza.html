Gyoza

Description

Gyoza/dumplings/dimsum are variations of asian dumplings. This recipes is originally off of budgetbytes.


Ingredients

double batch

  • 4 cups of finely shredded cabbage
  • 1 Tbsp of canola or vegetable oil
  • 3 green onions
  • 3 cloves of garlic/1 Tbsp minced
  • 1 Tbsp grated ginger
  • 1.5 Tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 1.5 lb ground pork
  • 50 wonton/gyoza wrappers

Steps

  1. Chop the 4 cups of cabbage finely (about half a cabbage)
  2. saute the cabbage in a pan over medium heat with a bit of oil until it's wilted to half its original volume.
  3. Transfer the cabbage to a large bowl.
  4. Chop up the green onions, garlic, and grate the ginger.
  5. Mix the green onions, garlic, ginger, pork, soy sauce, and sesame oil all into the cabbage.
  6. Massage the mixture with your hands until it's mixed evenly. It should be slightly sticky or tacky to the touch.
  7. Spoon about a tsp of the mixture into the middle of the wonton wrapper.
  8. Wet the edges of the wrapper (dip your finger into some water and run it over the edges).
  9. Fold the wrapper over the filling and pinch the edges to seal.
  10. When ready to cook the gyoza, heat 1 Tbsp canola or vegetable oil in a non-stick skillet over medium heat. Working in batches, add the gyoza with the seam side up, and fry until the bottom is golden brown and crispy. Using the lid as a shield, pour about 1/4 to 1/3 cup water into the skillet and quickly place the lid on top to contain the steam and splatter. Let the gyoza steam in the skillet for 1-2 minutes, or until most of the liquid has boiled away. Remove the lid and let the gyoza cook for a minute more, or until the bottom is crispy again. Serve hot.

Note, the gyoza are usually served with a sauce made out of a 2:1 ratio of soy sauce:rice vinegar. Also, you can add red pepper flakes for a nice kick.
